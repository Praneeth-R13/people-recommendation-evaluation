# Summary
The project involves the design and construction of a new 43,584 gross square feet (GSF) Texas A&M AgriLife Rio Grande Valley Research Center at McAllen. This facility will be located on the Texas A&M University Higher Education Center at McAllen (HECM) campus. The primary objectives are to provide state-of-the-art research capabilities for various programs, including open laboratories, dedicated support spaces, and shared laboratory support, with a focus on long-term flexibility and biosecurity for food systems. Additionally, the facility will include student center spaces to foster campus connection, support student services, and accommodate enrollment growth.
The new center will expand Texas A&M AgriLife Research's initiatives in the Rio Grande Valley, offering clinical research capabilities for the Institute of Advancing Health through Agriculture (IHA), community-based research facilities like a fitness room and studio kitchen, and a fabrication center for developing sensors and controls for food system biosecurity.


# Scope
The project scope includes the selection of an Architect/Engineer (A/E) firm for the design of the new Texas A&M AgriLife Rio Grande Valley Research Center. The facility will total 43,584 GSF (25,279 ASF) and will encompass new construction. The A/E firm will be responsible for providing professional services for the entire project, which includes designing research spaces (open laboratories, procedure rooms, equipment space, shared laboratory support, clinical exam rooms, fitness room, studio kitchen, fabrication center) and student center spaces (commons areas, flag room, gaming space, food service space, retail storefront, and an office suite for student services). The planning amount for the project is $53,496,884, with an Amount Available for Construction Contract (AACC) of $40,122,679. The A/E firm will be expected to provide services in specialties such as Architect, AV & IT, Civil, Lab Planner, MEP, Security, and Structural.
